Environmental Education

Understand the science behind environmental impact metrics and why they matter for a sustainable future.

CO₂ Emissions & Climate Impact

Carbon dioxide (CO₂) is the primary greenhouse gas contributing to climate change. In product lifecycle analysis, CO₂ emissions span raw material extraction, manufacturing, transportation, use, and disposal — collectively called the "carbon footprint."

Electronics dominate: a single laptop emits 200 kg CO₂ during manufacturing alone — equivalent to driving a car for over 800 km. The digital economy accounts for approximately 4% of global greenhouse gas emissions.

  • Laptop manufacturing: 200 kg CO₂ (highest in our dataset)
  • Smartphone: 70 kg CO₂ per device manufactured
  • Paper Notebook: Only 1 kg CO₂ — 200x less than a laptop
  • 1.5°C Paris Agreement target requires 45% CO₂ reduction by 2030
  • Choosing LED bulbs over incandescent saves 40 kg CO₂ per year per bulb

Water Footprint in Production

The "water footprint" of a product encompasses all freshwater consumed in its entire supply chain — from growing raw materials to manufacturing processes. This hidden water consumption is rarely visible to consumers but represents a critical sustainability concern.

The fashion industry is among the worst offenders: producing one cotton T-shirt consumes 2,700 litres of water — the same as drinking water for one person for 2.5 years. This invisible water cost is a major driver of water scarcity in cotton-growing regions.

  • Cotton T-Shirt: 2,700 L — equivalent to 25 bathtubs of water
  • Laptop manufacturing: 1,500 L of virtual water embedded
  • Glass Bottle: Only 6 L — 450x less than a cotton T-shirt
  • Agriculture accounts for 70% of global freshwater withdrawals
  • Water stress affects 40% of the global population today

Electronic Waste Crisis

E-waste is the world's fastest-growing waste stream. Global e-waste generation reached 57.4 million tonnes in 2021, yet only 17.4% was formally collected and recycled. The rest ends up in landfills or informal recycling operations, releasing toxic materials including lead, mercury, cadmium, and flame retardants.

The problem is accelerating as device lifespans decrease and consumer electronics penetration grows. 91.7% of survey respondents use smartphones — each device containing dozens of toxic and rare earth elements requiring careful end-of-life management.

  • 57.4 million tonnes of e-waste generated globally per year
  • Only 17.4% of e-waste is formally recycled
  • A smartphone contains up to 60 different elements from the periodic table
  • E-waste contains 1,000+ toxic substances including lead and mercury
  • Proper recycling of 1 million smartphones recovers 35 kg of gold, 350 kg silver
  • Repair and refurbishment can extend device life by 3-5 years
E-WASTE AWARENESS SURVEY
50% of respondents identified electronics as a major waste source, while 83.3% flagged plastic bottles — both reflecting significant public concern about these waste categories.

Plastic Pollution & Solutions

Plastic pollution is the most visible environmental crisis of our time. Over 380 million tonnes of plastic are produced annually, with only 9% ever recycled. The remaining 91% either ends up in landfills, is incinerated, or leaks into natural environments including oceans.

Our survey confirms public perception matches scientific data: 83.3% of respondents identified plastic bottles as the biggest waste generator. Single-use plastics have recyclability rates as low as 10%, making behavior change and material substitution the most effective interventions.

  • 8 million tonnes of plastic enter the ocean every year
  • Plastic shopping bags have a recyclability rate of only ~10%
  • A plastic bag takes 500–1,000 years to decompose
  • Cloth bags need to be reused just 11 times to break even environmentally
  • 66.7% of survey respondents already use reusable bags — a positive trend
  • Microplastics have been found in human blood, lungs, and placentas
PLASTIC vs ALTERNATIVES
Key Action: Switching from single-use plastic to cloth shopping bags reduces waste impact by 87% and energy consumption by 70%.
